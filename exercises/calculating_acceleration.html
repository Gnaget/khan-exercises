Alice was driving in a car down a straight road at start_speed km/h direction. She accelerates at a constant rate for time seconds, and ends up at end_speed in the same direction. What was her rate of acceleration in meters per second per second.

Acceleration can be calculated with the formula: acceleration = \dfrac{\Delta speed}{\Delta time}

Substitude in the known variables: acceleration = \dfrac{(end_speed - start_speed) km/h}{time seconds}

The resultant value is in units \dfrac{\dfrac{km}{hour}}{second}

To convert to feet per seconds: \dfrac{acceleration km}{hr*s} * \dfrac{1 hr}{3600s} * \dfrac{1000m}{1km}
